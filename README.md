# ***Global Health & Development Analytics Dashboard***

An interactive dashboard designed to analyze global health indicators across developed and developing countries. The dashboard visualizes multiple metrics such as life expectancy, GDP, infant mortality, healthcare expenditure, and disease-related deaths, providing a comprehensive view of global development trends.

## Project Overview

This dashboard enables users to explore and compare health and development metrics across countries and over time. With filters for Year, Country, and Status (Developed/Developing), users can quickly gain insights into global health patterns and socioeconomic disparities.

## Tools Used

Microsoft Excel / Power BI (based on your screenshot)

Pivot Charts & Tables

Slicers (Year, Country, Status)

Conditional Formatting

Interactive Visual Design

## Key Features

### **Life Expectancy Trend**

Displays year-wise improvement in average life expectancy.

Highlights highest and lowest life expectancy values.

### **GDP & Healthcare Expenditure**

Comparison of GDP between developed and developing nations.

Visual analysis of healthcare expenditure percentage.

### **Infant Mortality Analysis**

Pie chart showcasing Top 5 countries by infant death rate.

Total average infant deaths shown on KPI cards.

 ### **Disease Impact Breakdown**

Bar chart showing the burden of diseases such as Measles, Hepatitis B, HIV/AIDS, Polio, and Diphtheria across years.

### **Country Comparison**

Side-by-side comparison of Life Expectancy vs GDP for selected countries.

## Insights Highlighted

Developed countries show significantly higher GDP and life expectancy.

Developing countries face higher infant mortality rates.

Steady rise in life expectancy from 2000 to 2015.

Certain diseases (HIV/AIDS, Measles, Diphtheria) contribute heavily to health burdens in specific regions.

Healthcare spending shows strong correlation with improved life expectancy.

## Conclusion

The Global Health & Development Dashboard provides a clear visualization of key health indicators, enabling users to:

Identify global development gaps

Understand health improvement trends

Compare developed vs developing nations

Support data-driven policy and healthcare decisions
